About

Within the US, the Electoral College is the process that determines the presidency every four years instead of a direct vote. A confusing process that dates all the way back to the Constitutional Convention, its place within elections seem to hold less relevance as time passes on.

Issues

The Electoral College, in a stunningly anti-democratic way, has gone against the popular vote five times within US history due to its mechanisms. But the Electoral College is also plagued with other issues that further take away the voice of the people.
